# How to Find Accessibility Settings - Step by Step

## Method 1: Search in Settings (Easiest!)

1. **In the Settings app**, look at the **top** of the screen
2. You should see a **search bar** or **magnifying glass icon** üîç
3. **Tap the search bar**
4. **Type**: `accessibility` (all lowercase)
5. **Tap** on "Accessibility" in the search results
6. You should now see the Accessibility settings page

---

## Method 2: Scroll Through Settings Categories

1. **In the Settings app**, look at the **left side** (the list of categories)
2. **Scroll down** through the list
3. Look for one of these:
   - **"Accessibility"** (with an icon that looks like a person or accessibility symbol)
   - **"System"** ‚Üí then tap it ‚Üí then look for "Accessibility" inside
   - **"Device"** ‚Üí then tap it ‚Üí then look for "Accessibility" inside

---

## Method 3: Different Android Versions

Depending on your Android version, Accessibility might be in different places:

### Android 12+ (newer):
- Settings ‚Üí **Accessibility** (directly in main list)

### Android 11 and older:
- Settings ‚Üí **System** ‚Üí **Accessibility**
- OR Settings ‚Üí **Device** ‚Üí **Accessibility**

Once you find Accessibility settings, you should see:

1. A list of **"Downloaded apps"** or **"Installed services"**
2. In that list, you should see **"Vibe"** (or "vibe")
3. **Tap on "Vibe"**
4. **Toggle the switch to ON**
